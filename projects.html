Evaluating the Usability and Performance of the Lighthouse V2 Ground Truth System

My seminar project during the master in information and computer engineering.

Summary:
The seminar project aimed to benchmark the LighthouseV2 indoor localization system, specifically focusing on evaluating its performance and accuracy. Indoor localization plays a crucial role in various domains, including retail, logistics, and healthcare, where precise tracking of people and assets is essential.
Different metrics like accuracy, precision, update rate, latency, robustness, and scalability were evaluated during the project. By knowing these performance boundaries of the system, it can then be used as a ground truth for Wi-Fi-based, Bluetooth-based, or Ultra-Wide-Band-based localization systems.
This project was furthermore the starting point for my master thesis.

September, 2022

Exploiting Fine Grained CFI for Data Integrity

My bachelor project in information and computer engineering.

Summary:
The bachelor project focused on developing and implementing a data integrity extension to enhance an existing control flow integrity (CFI) mechanism. Control flow integrity ensures that a program follows its intended execution path, thereby protecting it from control flow hijacking attacks. However, traditional CFI solutions primarily focus on safeguarding control transfers while neglecting data manipulation vulnerabilities.
The project aimed to address this limitation by introducing a data integrity extension for the existing FIPAC control flow integrity. The extension was designed to protect compile time known data structures from unauthorized modifications or tampering during runtime. By ensuring the integrity of data, the project aimed to provide comprehensive security coverage against sophisticated control flow attacks that exploit data manipulation vulnerabilities.
The project's findings contribute to the field of information security by expanding the scope of protection offered from FIPAC CFI, ultimately improving the resilience of software systems.

April, 2017

Building a race kart

Our graduation project from the higher technical collage.

Summary:
Our graduation project at the Higher Technical College involved a team of four dedicated individuals who embarked on the ambitious endeavor of building a race kart from scratch. Our aim was to construct a high-performance vehicle that could push the boundaries of speed and acceleration. Throughout the project, each team member contributed their unique expertise, and my specific role focused on visualization, cabling, drive train design, and mechanics.
Our race kart was a testament to our collective passion for engineering and innovation. By meticulously designing and assembling each component, we aimed to achieve optimal performance and safety. The kart's remarkable speed was a significant accomplishment, as it could reach an impressive top speed of 120 km/h. Moreover, it exhibited exceptional acceleration capabilities, reaching 80 km/h from a standstill in just 3.6 seconds.
The experience not only provided us with valuable hands-on engineering knowledge but also showcased our ability to work as a team, overcome challenges, and deliver exceptional results. Furthermore we were able to win the first price of Austria's Energy Award for Matura Projects.
